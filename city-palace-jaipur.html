Indian Palaces

City Palace Jaipur

The Royal Residence of the Maharajas

Introduction

City Palace Jaipur is one of the most magnificent royal residences in India. Located in the heart of Jaipur, Rajasthan, the palace complex is a blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural styles. It remains the residence of the royal family of Jaipur and is one of the most visited attractions in the Pink City.

History of City Palace Jaipur

The City Palace was constructed by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in the early 18th century when he shifted his capital from Amber to Jaipur. Over the years, successive rulers added new structures, courtyards, gardens, and decorative elements. The palace complex consists of Mubarak Mahal, Chandra Mahal, Diwan-i-Khas, and Diwan-i-Aam.

The Chandra Mahal still serves as the residence of the royal family, while other parts are open to visitors as museums displaying royal costumes, weapons, manuscripts, and artifacts.

Architecture

The palace showcases a beautiful fusion of Mughal and Rajput architecture. It features intricately carved doorways, marble courtyards, colorful frescoes, and delicate latticework. The famous Peacock Gate inside the palace is one of the most photographed spots and represents the beauty of Indian craftsmanship.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 9:30 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹200 (Indian), ₹700 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar, Albert Hall Museum
