Amber Fort

Complete History, Architecture & Travel Guide

Introduction

Amber Fort is one of the most beautiful and historic forts in India, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built with red sandstone and marble, this magnificent fort stands proudly on a hill overlooking Maota Lake. It reflects the grandeur of Rajput architecture and attracts millions of visitors every year.

History of Amber Fort

Amber Fort was built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, a trusted general of Mughal Emperor Akbar. Later rulers expanded the fort and added beautiful palaces and courtyards. Before Jaipur city was built, Amber Fort served as the main capital of the Kachwaha Rajputs.

Architecture

The fort showcases a blend of Hindu and Mughal architecture. Major attractions include:

  • Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace)
  • Diwan-e-Aam (Hall of Public Audience)
  • Diwan-e-Khas (Hall of Private Audience)
  • Ganesh Pol Gate

The intricate mirror work in Sheesh Mahal is especially famous and creates a magical glow when light reflects.

Timings & Entry Fee

  • Open: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Light & Sound Show: Evening

  • Foreign Visitors: Approx ₹500

Note: Prices may change.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Amber Fort is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing.
