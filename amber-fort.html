Amber Fort

The Magnificent Rajput Palace of Jaipur

Introduction

Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is one of the most beautiful and historic forts in Rajasthan. Located near Jaipur, it reflects the grand lifestyle of Rajput rulers. Built with red sandstone and marble, the fort stands proudly on a hill overlooking Maota Lake. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most visited attractions in India.

History of Amber Fort

Amber Fort was built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I. Later rulers expanded the fort, adding magnificent palaces, courtyards, temples, and gardens. The fort served as the main residence of Rajput Maharajas before Jaipur city was established. The architecture beautifully combines Rajput and Mughal styles.

Architecture

The fort is famous for its Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Diwan-e-Aam, Diwan-e-Khas, and stunning courtyards. The intricate mirror work and carvings demonstrate advanced craftsmanship. The fort walls and gates are designed for defense, while interiors reflect royal luxury.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹100 (Indian), ₹500 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
