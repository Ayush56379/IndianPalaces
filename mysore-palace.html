Mysore Palace (Amba Vilas Palace)

History, Architecture & Visitor Information

Introduction

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most magnificent royal residences in India. Located in Mysuru, Karnataka, it serves as the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty. The palace is one of the most visited tourist attractions in India after the Taj Mahal.

History of Mysore Palace

The original palace was built in the 14th century but was destroyed multiple times due to wars and fire accidents. The present structure was completed in 1912 after a fire destroyed the earlier wooden palace in 1897.

The new palace was designed by British architect Henry Irwin in the Indo-Saracenic style. It beautifully combines Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic architectural elements.

The palace remains the cultural center of Mysuru, especially during the famous Dasara festival when it is illuminated with thousands of lights.

Architecture & Design

Mysore Palace is built using granite stone with deep pink marble domes. The structure features multiple arches, balconies, and tall towers.

  • Grand Durbar Hall
  • Royal Private Chambers
  • Golden Throne
  • Stained Glass Ceilings
  • Intricate Wooden Doors

The interiors are decorated with detailed carvings, colorful paintings, and Belgian stained glass that reflect royal grandeur.

Timings & Entry Fee

  • Open: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Indian Visitors: Approx ₹70
  • Foreign Visitors: Approx ₹500
  • Illumination: Sundays & Public Holidays (Evening)

Note: Prices may change.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mysore Palace is between October and February when the weather is pleasant. If possible, plan your visit during the Dasara festival to witness the grand celebrations.

Travel Tips

  • Arrive early to avoid long queues.
  • Photography is restricted inside some sections.
  • Visit nearby Chamundi Hills and Brindavan Gardens.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking.
