Indian Palaces

Mysore Palace

The Royal Residence of the Wadiyar Dynasty

Introduction

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most magnificent royal residences in India. Located in Mysuru, Karnataka, this grand palace attracts millions of visitors every year. It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and stands as a symbol of royal heritage and architectural brilliance.

History of Mysore Palace

The original palace was built in the 14th century but was destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. The current structure was completed in 1912 after a fire destroyed the earlier wooden palace. Designed by British architect Henry Irwin, the palace reflects Indo-Saracenic architectural style. During the Dussehra festival, the palace is illuminated with over 100,000 lights, creating a breathtaking view.

Architecture

Mysore Palace is built using granite with pink marble domes. The structure combines Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic architectural styles. The Durbar Hall, Kalyana Mantapa, and intricately designed ceilings are major highlights. The stained glass windows, carved doors, and golden throne add to its royal grandeur.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Mysuru, Karnataka
  • Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹70 (Indian), ₹500 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to February
  • Special Attraction: Dussehra Festival Illumination
