Jaigarh Fort

The Fort of Victory & Home of Jaivana Cannon

Introduction

Jaigarh Fort is one of the strongest forts of Rajasthan, located on the Aravalli hills overlooking Amber Fort in Jaipur. Built mainly for defense purposes, the fort is famous for housing the world’s largest cannon on wheels — the Jaivana Cannon.

History of Jaigarh Fort

Jaigarh Fort was built in 1726 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II to protect Amber Fort and its palace complex. The fort was strategically constructed to serve as a military stronghold. It contains underground water tanks, secret passages, and strong walls designed to withstand attacks.

The fort played a crucial role in safeguarding royal treasures and served as a storage facility for weapons and artillery.

The Jaivana Cannon

The Jaivana Cannon is the main attraction of Jaigarh Fort. Built in 1720, it is considered one of the largest cannons ever constructed on wheels. Although it was never used in warfare, it represents the engineering excellence of that time.

Architecture

The architecture of Jaigarh Fort reflects military planning and strength. The fort features watchtowers, massive gateways, courtyards, and water reservoirs. The red sandstone structure blends beautifully with the rugged hills of Jaipur.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹150 (Indian), ₹500 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Amber Fort, Nahargarh Fort
