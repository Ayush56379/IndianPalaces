Hampi Palaces

The Royal Complex of the Vijayanagara Empire

Introduction

Hampi Palaces are part of the magnificent ruins of Hampi in Karnataka. This UNESCO World Heritage Site represents the glorious capital of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Once one of the richest cities in the world during the 14th–16th centuries, Hampi is today known for its grand temples, royal enclosures, and unique stone architecture.

History of Hampi

Hampi became the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century. Under rulers like Krishnadevaraya, the empire reached its peak in power, trade, and cultural development.

The city was a major trading center attracting merchants from Persia, Portugal, and other regions.

In 1565, the empire fell after the Battle of Talikota, and the city was destroyed, leaving behind the beautiful ruins we see today.

Famous Palace Structures

  • Lotus Mahal – Indo-Islamic style royal pavilion
  • Elephant Stables – Massive domed chambers
  • Queen’s Bath – Royal bathing complex
  • Royal Enclosure – Administrative center
  • Hazara Rama Temple – Detailed Ramayana carvings

Architecture

Hampi architecture is a blend of Dravidian temple style and Islamic influences. Granite stone was widely used, giving structures a massive and durable appearance.

Intricate carvings, open pavilions, and symmetrical designs showcase advanced planning and artistic excellence.
