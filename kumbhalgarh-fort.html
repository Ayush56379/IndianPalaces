About Kumbhalgarh Fort

Kumbhalgarh Fort is located in Rajsamand district of Rajasthan. It is famous for having the second-longest continuous wall in the world after the Great Wall of China.

History

Built in the 15th century by Maharana Kumbha, the fort served as a refuge for the rulers of Mewar. It is also the birthplace of Maharana Pratap.

Architecture

The fort stretches over 36 kilometers with massive defensive walls. Inside the fort, there are over 300 temples and several palaces.

UNESCO Heritage Site

Kumbhalgarh Fort is part of the Hill Forts of Rajasthan and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers pleasant weather for exploring the fort.

Entry Fee & Timings

Indian Visitors: ₹40 Foreign Visitors: ₹600 Timings: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
