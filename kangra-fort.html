Kangra Fort

The Ancient Guardian of Himachal Pradesh

Introduction

Kangra Fort is one of the oldest and largest forts in India. Located in Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh, the fort stands proudly on a hilltop overlooking the Banganga River.

With a history spanning more than 2000 years, Kangra Fort has witnessed countless battles, dynasties, and rulers. It remains a symbol of resilience and strength.

Historical Background

The fort was originally built by the Trigarta Kingdom mentioned in ancient texts. Over centuries, it was ruled by Rajputs, Mughals, Sikhs, and the British.

Maharaja Ranjit Singh captured Kangra Fort in 1809, making it part of the Sikh Empire. Later, the British took control after the Anglo-Sikh wars.

Architecture

Kangra Fort features massive stone walls, strong defensive gates, and temples inside the complex. The main gates include Ahani and Amiri Darwaza.

Inside the fort, visitors can see ancient temples such as Lakshmi Narayan Temple and Ambika Devi Temple.

Natural Setting

The fort is surrounded by beautiful hills and rivers, offering breathtaking views. The combination of history and natural beauty makes it a popular tourist attraction.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Kangra, Himachal Pradesh
  • Timings: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: Small entry charge
  • Best Time to Visit: March to June & September to November
  • Nearest Airport: Gaggal Airport
