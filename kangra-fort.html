About Kangra Fort

Kangra Fort is one of the oldest and largest forts in India. It is located near the town of Kangra in Himachal Pradesh and stands as a symbol of ancient Indian history.

History

The fort dates back to the Trigarta Kingdom mentioned in the Mahabharata. It has witnessed many invasions including attacks by Mahmud of Ghazni and later Mughal rulers.

Architecture

Built on a steep cliff overlooking the Banganga and Patal Ganga rivers, the fort features massive stone walls, temples, gateways and scenic viewpoints.

Major Attractions

• Jahangiri Gate • Lakshmi Narayan Temple • Ambika Devi Temple • Scenic River Viewpoints

Best Time to Visit

March to June and September to November are ideal months due to pleasant weather.

Entry Fee & Timings

Entry Fee: ₹150 (Approx. for Indians, may vary) Timings: 9 AM to 5 PM
