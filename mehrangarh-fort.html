Mehrangarh Fort

The Pride of Jodhpur, Rajasthan

Introduction

Mehrangarh Fort is one of the largest and most magnificent forts in India. Located in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, the fort stands proudly on a rocky hill about 400 feet above the city. It offers breathtaking views of the famous “Blue City” of Jodhpur.

History of Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort was founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur. The fort was built to replace the older Mandore Fort and to establish a stronger defensive position.

Over the centuries, many rulers of the Rathore dynasty expanded and strengthened the fort. Each ruler contributed to its palaces, courtyards, and decorative structures.

Legends say that a hermit was displaced during construction and cursed the fort. To counter the curse, a human sacrifice ritual was performed, and a temple was built inside the fort.

Architecture & Design

Mehrangarh Fort is built using massive red sandstone walls. Its thick walls, high gates, and strategic location made it nearly impossible to conquer.

  • Seven massive entrance gates
  • Intricately carved balconies (Jharokhas)
  • Beautiful courtyards
  • Detailed lattice windows
  • Royal museum collections

The fort houses several palaces including Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace), Phool Mahal (Flower Palace), and Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), each decorated with gold paintings and mirror work.

Mehrangarh Museum

The museum inside the fort displays royal costumes, weapons, paintings, and artifacts belonging to the Rathore dynasty.

It is considered one of the best-preserved fort museums in India.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mehrangarh Fort is between October and March when the weather in Rajasthan is pleasant.

Avoid peak summer months as temperatures can be extremely high.

Travel Tips

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes.
  • Carry water during summer visits.
  • Visit early morning for better photography.
  • Do not miss sunset views from the fort walls.
