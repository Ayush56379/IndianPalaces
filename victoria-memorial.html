Victoria Memorial

The Marble Jewel of Kolkata

Introduction

Victoria Memorial is one of the most famous landmarks in Kolkata. Built in memory of Queen Victoria, this grand white marble monument stands as a reminder of British colonial rule in India.

Today, it functions as a museum and major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from across the world.

Historical Background

The foundation stone of Victoria Memorial was laid in 1906, and construction was completed in 1921. It was designed by Sir William Emerson in Indo-Saracenic architectural style.

The monument was funded by British officials and Indian princes to honor Queen Victoria after her death in 1901.

Architectural Highlights

The structure is built using white Makrana marble, similar to the Taj Mahal. It features a large central dome topped with the Angel of Victory statue.

  • Grand central dome
  • Beautiful marble sculptures
  • Large landscaped gardens
  • British and Mughal design elements
  • Rich museum galleries

The surrounding gardens make it one of the most peaceful and scenic places in Kolkata.

Museum & Galleries

The memorial houses several galleries displaying paintings, sculptures, rare photographs, manuscripts, and artifacts from the British era.

It provides insight into colonial history, freedom movement, and cultural heritage.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Kolkata, West Bengal
  • Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM (Closed Mondays)
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹30–₹200 depending on gallery
  • Best Time to Visit: October to February
  • Nearest Metro: Maidan Metro Station
